BASIC GAME RULES

TURNS AND ROUNDS

Every player and hostile has 1 turn per round. The initiative que is the order in which players and hostiles take their turn. After the last player or hostile in the initiative que takes their turn, the round ends.

Phases of a turn are divided into movements, actions, and checks. At the beginning of a turn, a player gets 1 free action and 1 free movement. Checks are all interactions that do not require rolls, such as asking for a description of a room or talking. During a turn, players may make more movements and take more actions at the cost of Action Points (AP) this is called pushing.

USEFUL ACRONYMS

  • EoT: End of the turn
  • EoR: End of the round
  • EoD: End of the day


MOVEMENT TYPES

There are 3 speeds of player movement: walking, running, and sprinting. Each square of the game board represents a 5x5 area. Walking moves the player 1 square, running moves the player 2 squares, and sprinting moves the player 3 squares. The speed of movement affects the player’s stance during the turn at the end of the turn. Every movement after the free movement costs 1 AP tier.


ACTION TYPES

Actions are any feats that use dice to determine the outcome.

FREE ACTIONS- The first action of a turn is always free. Examples include initiating combat, using skills, unlocking or hacking doors, scavenging or looting.

PUSH ACTIONS- Any actions past the first free action are push actions. Every push costs an action unit tier.

SAVING ACTIONS- Saves are actions performed in retaliation to other entities’ turns. All saves are off-turn. Every save costs an action unit tier.


STANCES

A player's stance affects their melee and ranged combat rolls. Players may change their stance for free at the end of turn.

TIER SYSTEM

Movements, actions, and abilities can be performed on a player’s turn beyond the free actions. Players can push characters to perform more feats by using action units. Action units consist of AP and class-specific units. Pushed actions cost one action unit tier per push, with the first push costing 1 action unit, the second push costing 2 additional action units, the third costing 3 additional action units, and so on. Push tiers only accumulate on a turn-by-turn basis and reset at the end of a player’s turn. Saves made off-turn start back at tier one, costing 1 action unit for the first save and so on.

CLASS TOOLS

ELECTROMIZER

Portable, contained fusion reactor that allows for the guided use of electrical energy. All actions performed with this unit consume CH. The amount of CH varies per action and can be increased or decreased in some situations. Examples of objects an electromizer can interact with include: engines, robotics, energy weapons, and cybernetics. Characters that hold an electromizer can perform the following basic actions:

  • Add or remove CH from a system
  • Add or remove electrical power from a system
  • Activate objects that require CH
  • Detonate electromizer

Classes that start with an electromizer are Chargers, Technomancers, Burners, and Reavers. These classes begin the game with a makeshift electromizer (See “Item Quality”).


COMMAND UNIT

Portable drone control unit used for commanding swarms of nanites. All actions performed with this unit consume DR. The amount of DR varies per action and can be increased or decreased in some situations. Examples of objects drones can interact with include: the environment, players, enemies, machines, and other drones. Characters that hold a drone unit can perform the following basic actions:

  • Disassemble or assemble objects
  • Heal health or armor points
  • Basic drone attack
  • Basic drone counterattack

Comand unit actions use the operator effective range table unless stated otherwise. Classes that start with a command unit are Operators, Burners, Shifters, and Swarmers. These classes begin the game with a makeshift command unit (See “Item Quality”).


ETHER KEY

Portable, wireless super computer that can access the Ether. All actions performed with this unit consume CR. The amount of CR varies per action and can be increased or decreased in some situations. With no Ether access, Ether keys have a functional range of 10 meters. Examples of objects Ether keys can interact with include: the Ether, Ether-connected devices, computers, and cybernetics. Characters that hold an Ether key can perform the following basic actions:

  • Access computer systems
  • Access the Ether
  • Access devices
  • Access cybernetic

Classes that start with an Ether key are Hackers, Technomancers, Intruders, and Shifters. These classes begin the game with a makeshift ether key (See “Item Quality”).

Accuracy One-Handed Melee Weapons- Every level in this skill grants +1 to melee attack rolls with small and medium class melee weapons.

Accuracy Two-Handed Melee Weapons- Every level in this skill grants +1 to melee attack rolls with melee weapons standard class or larger.

Accuracy Common Weapon Types- Every level in this skill grants +1 to attack rolls of common weapon types.

Accuracy Uncommon Weapon Types- Every level in this skill grants +1 to attack rolls of uncommon weapon types.

Accuracy Rare Weapon Types- Every level in this skill grants +1 to attack rolls of rare weapon types.

Accuracy Exotic Weapon Types- Every level in this skill grants +1 to attack rolls of exotic weapon types.

Accuracy Thrown Weapons- Every level in this skill grants +1 to attack rolls made with thrown object.

Accuracy Unarmed- Every level in this skill grants +1 to attack rolls made with bare hands or offensive apparel.

Agility- Every level in Agility grants +1 to rolls requiring physical dexterity.

Charisma- Every level in Charisma grants +1 to rolls requiring communication.

Endurance- Every level in Endurance increases AP capacity by 1.

Intelligence- Every level in Intelligence grants +1 to rolls requiring accumulated knowledge.

Scavenge (Basic)- Every level in Scavenge grants +1 to scavenging rolls.

Serpentine (Terminal)- All saving rolls made against ranged attacks gain a +1 modifier if running or sprinting.

Slippery (Terminal)- All saving rolls made while sneaking gain a +1 modifier.

Strength- Every level in Strength grants +1 to rolls requiring physical might.

Willpower- Every level in Willpower grants +1 to rolls requiring mental fortitude.

Wisdom- Every level in Wisdom grants +1 to rolls requiring innate knowledge.


ACTIVE

Equipment Melee- Spend 1 AP to attempt a small weapon melee attack using a piece of equipped apparel.

First Aid (Basic)- Consume first aid gear to attempt to restore health points. Every level in First Aid grants +1 to first aid rolls.

Repair (Basic)- Consume scrap to attempt to repair armor points. Every level in Repair grants +1 to repair rolls.
